Ema Datshi

Description

Considered Bhutan's national dish, Ema Datshi consists of spicy chili peppers simmered in a melted cheese sauce. It's often eaten with red rice and is a flavorful, fiery delight.

Ingredients

  • 5-6 hot chili peppers (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Water
  • Cooking oil
  • 200g of Bhutanese cheese or any hard cheese (like cheddar)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped

Steps

  1. Cut the chili peppers lengthwise and remove the seeds if you prefer less heat. Slice them thinly.
  2. Heat a little oil in a pan and saut√© the chopped onions until they turn translucent
  3. Add the sliced chili peppers and chopped tomatoes to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Cut the cheese into small cubes and add it to the pan.
  5. Stir well and let it simmer on low heat until the cheese melts, forming a creamy sauce.
  6. Add salt to taste and adjust the consistency by adding water if it's too thick.
  7. Serve hot with red rice.
